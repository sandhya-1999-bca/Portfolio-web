Overview

Why the site needed a redesign

Verveo is a global UX and digital engineering company. Their existing website (verveo.design) was built on WordPress and didn't reflect the quality of their work. The visual identity felt generic, page structure was minimal, and there were no service-focused pages to support business growth.

I was brought in to lead the complete UI redesign — starting from a brand new visual language through to designing new pages, custom illustrations, and SVG animations that now run across the live site at verveo.com.

Key Learnings

What this project taught me

01

Design is brand strategy

Every visual decision — colour, space, motion — communicates positioning. UI design is not decoration; it's how a business speaks without words.

02

SVG animation needs craft and restraint

I learned to balance expressiveness with performance. The most effective animations were purposeful and subtle — not distracting, but memorable.

03

Designing systems, not just screens

Designing 10+ pages forced me to think in components. Consistency at scale requires decisions made early and enforced across every page.

04

Real constraints sharpen creativity

Unlike side projects, this was a live brand with real stakeholders. I learned to balance creative ambition with business needs and dev feasibility.
